Speakers

Mimi Khúc

The Revolution is in the Heart

We know that much of how we think comes from what we encounter and learn from our social environment. Knowledge as deeply socialized. But what about our feelings? Are our feelings our own unique experiences? Or are they shaped and constrained by social and cultural scripts? This talk explores how many of us live tightly scripted emotional lives, with dire consequences not only for ourselves but for those around us as well. And it explores the ways that our feeling lives are a critical dimension of what it means to be human--but also what it means to be political and ethical. In our feeling lives, we can both trace the ghostly presence of power and find new forms of political agency. The revolution is in the critical workings of the heart as much as anywhere else.

Misti Burmeister

The Purpose of Pain—Using Rage to Heal

Few of us have been raised in environments where we learned how to process emotional trauma. Finding a safe space to share our fears—sadness, anger, grief—is nearly impossible in a society that has little capacity to process painful emotions.

But the purpose of pain is to help us grow, and it’s rarely easy. To do such healing alone is nearly impossible, yet to share emotional pain is risky.

“What if they think I’m losing it and run the other way?” we think, as we put a smile on our face, secretly wishing it were safe to get real. Of course it’s not safe and it never will be—not until we give ourselves permission to process and evolve through our pain.

While we will never be able to stop our brains from rehashing the stories that prompt such pain, we can become aware of them. And that awareness of the difference between what’s happening and the story we tell ourselves is our epicenter of personal power.

This process of distinguishing the difference between our internal stories and the truth is at the center of personal freedom. It is the reason to meditate, chant, practice yoga, get in nature, play your guitar, sculpt your lawn, paint, or tinker with your car in the garage. It allows us to quiet the external noise long enough to hear what we’re saying to ourselves.
