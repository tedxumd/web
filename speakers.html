Mimi Khúc

The Revolution is in the Heart

We know that much of how we think comes from what we encounter and learn from our social environment. Knowledge as deeply socialized. But what about our feelings? Are our feelings our own unique experiences? Or are they shaped and constrained by social and cultural scripts? This talk explores how many of us live tightly scripted emotional lives, with dire consequences not only for ourselves but for those around us as well. And it explores the ways that our feeling lives are a critical dimension of what it means to be human--but also what it means to be political and ethical. In our feeling lives, we can both trace the ghostly presence of power and find new forms of political agency. The revolution is in the critical workings of the heart as much as anywhere else.

Misti Burmeister

The Purpose of Pain—Using Rage to Heal

Few of us have been raised in environments where we learned how to process emotional trauma. Finding a safe space to share our fears—sadness, anger, grief—is nearly impossible in a society that has little capacity to process painful emotions.

But the purpose of pain is to help us grow, and it’s rarely easy. To do such healing alone is nearly impossible, yet to share emotional pain is risky.

“What if they think I’m losing it and run the other way?” we think, as we put a smile on our face, secretly wishing it were safe to get real. Of course it’s not safe and it never will be—not until we give ourselves permission to process and evolve through our pain.

While we will never be able to stop our brains from rehashing the stories that prompt such pain, we can become aware of them. And that awareness of the difference between what’s happening and the story we tell ourselves is our epicenter of personal power.

This process of distinguishing the difference between our internal stories and the truth is at the center of personal freedom. It is the reason to meditate, chant, practice yoga, get in nature, play your guitar, sculpt your lawn, paint, or tinker with your car in the garage. It allows us to quiet the external noise long enough to hear what we’re saying to ourselves.

Zeinab Karake

Determinants of Successful Cyber Security Policies and Strategies in Emerging Countries

What should countries take into consideration when developing their cyber security policies and strategies? What factors should be incorporated in order to increase the success of enforcing those policies? Dr. Karake will share the findings of her research, covered in her forthcoming book, “Enforcing Cyber Security in Emerging Countries.” Specifically she will cover the results of the content analysis of national cyber security strategies of tens of developing and emerging countries; she will also discuss the guiding framework which she developed for understanding the determinants of the quality and comprehensiveness of National Cyber Security (NCS) policies and strategies in those countries from a resource-based theory perspective.

Evelyn Atieno

Starting A Movement Through Social Media

Ever wonder how #Blacklivesmatter become as big as it is now? It was all through social media. I started my magazine — Affinity Magazine— at 16 due to the lack of authentic teen voices in journalism on topics such as social justice and politics. Since then I have generated over 2 million views, over 400 teen writers, verified status on Twitter and over 39,000 followers. I have been featured on MTV's 2016 list of social media warriors and Business Insider's most impressive high school graduates list. My magazine has encouraged outlets to take teen writing seriously. Affinity Magazine is changing the world of digital media, and it's a movement teens are supporting 100%.

Kristoffer Dramby

Managing Meadows for Pollinators: Value-Added and Sustainable Benefits from Solar Fields

Kris is the Director of Energy and Natural Resources for VHB’s Mid-Atlantic region and a member of VHB’s Corporate Energy Team, providing support in the following energy market areas; renewables (solar, wind, biomass), electric and natural gas transmission and distribution. He has nearly two decades of professional experience in both the private and public sectors and is a recognized Certified Wildlife Biologist (CWB), Professional Wetland Scientist (PWS), and Certified Ecologist (CE) with extensive experience relating to the assessment of the natural systems. Kris has worked with both public and private sector clients in varying degrees of project scope, size, and complexity, under multiple federal- and state-level regulation requirements. His expertise includes wildlife and forest management; restoration ecology, ecosystem function, wetland ecology, Endangered Species Act and Clean Water Act compliance, environmental permitting, and composite mitigation planning. Kris is a graduate of Towson University with a B.S. in Biology, concentrating in Wildlife Ecology, and also holds a graduate certificate in Natural Resources Management and Policy from Virginia Polytechnic Institute and State University.

Steven Chen

It’s time to start eating ethically

Vegetarianism is increasingly becoming mainstream. Its health benefits have long been touted. New research also suggests its public health and environmental benefits. In his talk, Steven Chen calls for continued commitments to the diet, and suggests that we reconsider how we think about the diet.

Steven Chen is a volunteer with the Buddhist Tzu Chi Foundation, an international nonprofit aimed at reducing global suffering and empowering individuals and communities. For over 50 years, the philanthropy has provided disaster relief and medical aid to over 100 countries. As part of his work with the organization, Steven has facilitated workshops detailing the importance of service and exploring the intersection between faith, service, and philanthropy.
